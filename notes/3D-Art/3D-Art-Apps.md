

Active Apps Used For Authoring 3D Content
=========================================

Below is a list of the main apps all that 3D digital artists should probably know about. (Obviously there are many more, we only list the most important widespread or notable ones.)

Also see the 2D Apps page, since some mostly 2D apps have 3D features as well.


### FOSS

Blender
    .
    Now arguably the most popular 3D software (particularly if we include students and hobbiests) Blender contains a wealth of features and even contains additional functionality not usually included in 3D Authoring software, such as painting and illustraion (including 2D pixel and vector art/painting) Video Editing,Compositing for VFX, etc. As of 2026-02-28, blender is still less popular in the professional industry, but is rapidly gaining ground.
    


Commercial / Proprietary
---------------------------------------------

Autodesk Maya
    Generally the most professionally popular general purpose 3D authoring app. Maya is easy enough to use for artists who aren't particularly technical, but also is powerful enough to support workflows at major studios. Maya is widely used to create blockbuster films, pictures, triple-A games, etc.

ZBrush
    The most widespread sculpting/modeling software.

Houdini
    Excellent for highly complex procedural and technical workflows, less artist friendly.

Unreal Engine
    A very power engine for 3D content authoring that was historically used only as a game engine but now is more multipurpose.

Substance Modeler
    A procedural modeler, much like Houdini, but more tightly focused on modeling, not VFX.

Autodesk 3dsMax
  In many ways similar to Autodesk Maya, and used to be popular in a lot of studios, but it has really lost popularity in the film/animation/vfx/games industries, and is now used primarily in archetechtural visualization (as well as product viz).

Commercial / Proprietary
----------------------------------------------

Modo
   Powerful polygon and subdivision modeling package
   Still useful today (as of 2026), but discontinued

Softimage, and it's successor Softimage XSI
    Was popularly among professionals, but only ran on extremely expensive computers, by the SGI hardware company. Was used quite a bit for feature films, television, and video game graphics. Notably, Reboot, the first 3D animated television show, was created in Softimage.
    
3D Studio
    Was very popular in the 1990's, ran inside DOS

Lightwave
	Was very popular in the 1990's, ran on Amiga computers and was often used along with "Video Toaster", the video creation system for Amiga. Lightwave was used to create animation for the sci-fi series Babylon 5, which was one of the first live action television shows to feature significant amount of 3D CG for their VFX.
