Theory of 3D Computer Graphics
================================

Theoretical foundations of 3D computer graphics, covering the concepts that
underlie practical 3D/VFX work: geometry, surface types, UV mapping, topology,
and related topics.


---


Articles
---------

- [[Understanding-3D-Computer-Graphics]] -- vertices, edges, polygons, normals,
  elements, hierarchy, pivot points, object mode and component mode. Based on
  "Understanding 3D Computer Graphics" by Joe Crawford (Teaching3D).

- [[UV-Mapping-Theory]] -- four-part series on UV mapping coordinates, texture
  space, seams, islands, stretching, projection methods, pelt mapping, UV
  layout, and workflow tips. By Joe Crawford (Teaching3D).

- [[Polygon-Meshes]] -- types of polygon meshes (base meshes, subdiv cage
  models, optimized/low-poly, complex representations like Nanite), quad
  triangulation rules, and the principle of justifying every mesh component.

- [[Topology-for-Subdiv]] -- topology concepts specific to subdivision surfaces:
  Catmull-Clark subdivision, poles and valence, stretching and tension.
